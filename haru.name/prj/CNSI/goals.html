Conceptual goals

Nano: breaking down things to their basic constituents to better understand the way they work; working with the smallest components

Addressing of Goals

Reflect CNSI

- Use of Nanotext: show visually how large, simplistic looking objects are composed of smaller components; how they can be broken down to their data and reassmbled into new forms
- Images and words may reflect nanotechnology

Reflect Studies @UCSB, MAT

- Nanotext is made of many different types of “text” reflecting the different fields of study at UCSB; e.g. English, Chinese, German, Mathematical Equations, Scientific Equations etc.
- Reflects the way that our larger structures of knowledge (symbolized by the bars) are composed of smaller data that can be studied in many different fields.
      - The breaking down of the bars symbolizes what the role of the university is to do: to break down “simple” beliefs and ways of knowing and creating new cultural/academic objects
      - Images and words also reflect UCSB

Reflect Physical Location

- Data collected from surrounding natural environment affects the image (speed, motion, color etc.)
- Colors of the bars and background reflect natural environment and continue color scheme from building

Reflect Venturi’s ideas

- Use of large bars and simple/bold words and images reflects Venturi’s style
